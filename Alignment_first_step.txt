1 Goal

The objective of this experiment is to empirically compare the behavior of Direct Preference
Optimization (DPO) and Kahneman-Tversky Optimization (KTO) in a continuous 1D
scalar world. We aim to demonstrate that DPO acts as a high-precision ranker that seeks to
maximize reward by collapsing the distribution onto the single best point (Greedy Maximization),
whereas KTO acts as a utility-based classifier that seeks to move the distribution into a "safe zone"
while preserving entropy (Satisficing).

Remark: ref KL Definition Clarification

The term ref KL in z(y) = h(y) - ref KL acts as a decision boundary shift and must be
operationally specified. Ambiguous choices include: a batch-estimated KL, a running-average
KL or a fixed scalar. For Gaussian policies, the KL divergence has a closed form. If used, it
should be stated explicitly, along with whether the KL is recomputed each iteration and whether
gradients flow through it. Implementations will diverge otherwise.
