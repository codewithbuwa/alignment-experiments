\section{Conclusion}
% ============================================
% Slide 10: Conclusion
% ============================================
\begin{frame}{Conclusion}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Greedy vs. Satisficing:} DPO acts as a high-precision ranker that collapses the distribution onto the best point to maximize reward. KTO acts as a utility-based classifier that moves the distribution into a ``safe zone'' while preserving entropy.
        \item \textbf{Boundary Seeking:} KTO behaves as a boundary seeker where the final policy spread ($\sigma$) scales with the width of the desirable zone $\delta$.
        \item \textbf{Entropy Dynamics:} DPO exhibits continuous ``entropy collapse'' as $\sigma \to 0$. KTO stabilizes once the distribution is safely within the boundaries.
        \item \textbf{Robustness:} KTO remains stable and effective even in imbalanced datasets (e.g., 10\% Good / 90\% Bad), whereas ranking-based methods like DPO may struggle without high-reward pairs.
        \item \textbf{Implementation Sensitivity:} The choice of KL estimation (analytical, batch-estimated, or running average) is a critical decision boundary shift that must be explicitly specified for reproducibility.
    \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
